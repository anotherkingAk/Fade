# About the Founder

</div>

Hi, I'm **Kypuvari Chandu** — people also call me **AK**.

I'm the founder of **ShadowCode**, a small company built on one simple
belief: the best tools come from actually caring about the problems they
solve, not just the technology behind them.

## Why I started this

I don't come from a background of easy resources. No large lab, no cluster
of GPUs sitting idle, no team of a hundred engineers — just a genuine pull
toward building things that matter, and the stubbornness to keep going
without those advantages.

**Fade** is part of that. It's an ambitious, real, working architecture for
a large-scale code model — built openly, from scratch, and shared freely,
because I believe tools like this shouldn't only exist behind the walls of
companies that already have everything they need to build them.

## Where things stand, honestly

I want to be upfront: Fade right now is an architecture, not a finished
trained model. Getting from here to something fully trained and running
takes real infrastructure and real compute — the kind of resources I don't
currently have on my own. That's the honest, unglamorous truth of building
something like this without a large company behind you.

But I'd rather be honest and keep building slowly than pretend to be
further along than I am. Every line of code in this repo is real. Every
step forward from here is one I intend to keep taking, for as long as it
takes.

## If this project means something to you

If Fade, or the idea behind it, resonates with you — if you believe small
teams and independent builders deserve a shot at building serious things,
not just big labs — I'd be genuinely grateful for your support. It doesn't
need to be much. Every bit helps this go from an architecture on paper to
something real and running, and it tells me someone out there believes this
is worth building.
